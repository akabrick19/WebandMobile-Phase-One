Welcome!

Where It All Began

The love for Labrador Retrivers started many years ago for Denise with a black lab mix named Scooter that they rescued from a junk yard. His previous name before they got him was "Sooner" since his previous owner would have rather seen him "sooner dead". His exact words when Denise took him was "Take him. He is stupid!" It was no surprise though that Scooter was anything but stupid and what his previous owner said about him was the farthest thing from the truth. He liked to play frisbee and when it would land accidently on top of the roof he would climb a ladder to retrieve it. Along with being a great retriever he was also a great babysitter for her toddlers at the time. When her son was the ages 4-6 he would open Scooters mouth and put his head in Scooters to pretend that Scooter was a lion. The toddlers are now adults but Scooter was a part of most of their childhood. The stories of Scooter could go on and on but in short he was a great dog!

First AKC Registered Labrador Retriever

Denises first AKC registered Labrador Retriever followed soon after Scooter. She was a yellow lab who they named Lil Bit due to the fact that she was the smallest one of the litter. Denise was able to be there for her birth and between the deliveries of the other puppies in the litter she ended up working on her for about 45 minutes to get her to respond and latch on to her mother. While Denise was doing all this her close friend who was the owner and breeder of the litter, was busy herself taking care of the whelping bitch. Now it should be mention that Denise did not want another dog but despite that it was 7 weeks later after Lil Bit was born that she came home. As the years went by a couple kids moved out and Denise married Dave Misaras in 2000. It was soon after that when her two kids, who were still living with her, along with Lil Bit and their cat, Dink, moved in with Dave. It was in 2004 that Denise made the difficult decision to have Lil Bit humanely euthanized due to the many health issues that she had. Unfortunately it was getting to a point where it was not in Lil Bits best interest to go through the rest of her life like she was. It was one of the hardest thing that Denise had to do and it was then that she told herself and Dave that she would never have another dog.

Finding Rain

The promise of never owning another dog after Lil Bit lasted all of 72 hours. After those few days of losing Lil Bit Denise started the journey to finding another AKC registered Labrador Retriever. It was certainly quite the task and took her over 7 months till she got in contact with Dale Brummett of Blackrock Labrador. Through many emails Dale let Denise know about Roger and Parke Dieckmeyer who had a litter that was sired by one of his dogs. It was from this litter of CH Blackrock Bravo to Barke's Shadow In The Night that Denise and Dave got second pick of the litter. Due to having Lil Bit Denise really wanted the one yellow girl that was in the litter but unfortanately that was Dales pick(Dale did have first pick). However, it all worked out in the end since the puppy that she did pick out turned out to be the true pick of the litter in Denises opinion. Rain was a Black Labrador Retriever and was definetely the queen of their lives! Rain was also the reason that Denise became no longer color blind to only wanting a yellow Labrador Retriever.

The Start of Showing

Eventually Denise and Dave became involved with the Huron River Labrador Retriever Club. Then eventually after that they started to show Rain. The members of the club helped Denise and Rain while they were first starting and despite just starting out Rain did really well. Showing Rain was what Denise did while Dave was out at fishing tournaments at the national and international level. Outside of fishing though Dave went along to the dog shows and the shows became almost as enjoyable for him as fishing was for him. Every now and then Dave will even take a dog into the show ring himself.

Their Breeding Program

Dave and Denise love their dogs and will put their dogs needs above everything else. Each of their dogs have different personalities and are unique in their own ways. In their eyes their dogs are pets first and foremost and working dogs second. They have many different veterinarians who they trust and are experts in their own special field, like hip and elbow specialist or eye specialist, along with trusted general veternarians. They will only breed their dogs when they are planning to keep at least one puppy to add to their family. It is due to this that they only have one or two litters a year. They take breeding very seriously and have a small list of stud dogs that they consider to be bred to their females. Before any dog becomes apart of their breeding program they need to have their health clearances. This includes but is not limited to the dogs hips, elbows, and eyes being tested before becoming apart of their breeding program. This ensures that quality Labrador Retrievers are being bred that have great temperaments and are able to do any kind of work from field to show ring to service. Denise and Dave love the show ring but they also enjoy doing field work as well which is why they strive to have multi purpose Labradors.

What To Expect With a Regen Labrador Retriever

Denise and Dave put a lot of time and effort into breeding Quality English Labrador Retrievers that are capable of doing whatever is required of them. Everyone should know that a Labrador Retriever is a fantastic family dog that can have tons of energy, especially as puppies! Their greatest joy is being challenged and having some kind of job to do. Through all the energy though they are very loving and loyal dogs who just want to have fun with their people. It is due to this that you can teach a lab to do almost anything and they will be as happy as ever! Anybody thinking of purchasing a Regen Labrador Retriever puppy should know that they will not go to their new homes until they are at least 8 weeks old. When a puppy goes to their new home they will have their first puppy vaccines, microchip and have been checked over by a vet. It is Denises and Daves hope that all their dogs, whether in the breeding program or not, live a long and happy life and are forever loved by the people around them.
